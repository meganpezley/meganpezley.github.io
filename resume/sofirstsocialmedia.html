The Problem

SOFIRST is a student organization at Iowa State University (ISU) that was reinstated in the fall of 2019. It is an alumni organization for students who were involved in FIRST robotics teams in elementary, middle school, or high school. Its goal is to connect ISU students with mentoring and event volunteering opportunities in the state. The organization was previously dissolved after a lack of involvement. We were worried about that happening again because we were a niche club. As a result, we decided to use our social media accounts to further build a community of FIRST alumni at ISU with the goal of helping other FIRST alumni and Iowa FIRST teams learn about our organization. As the Communication and Social Media Manager, I started and ran our social media accounts for 4 years.

1.

The Journey of Running the Social Media Accounts

I started Instagram and Twitter (now X) accounts because those accounts were popular among FIRST teams and college-aged students. For these accounts, our target audience was college students, and our secondary audience was FIRST teams.

I started by posting content about events, including announcements about upcoming SOFIRST events, recaps of previous SOFIRST events, information about volunteer opportunities, notes wishing teams good luck at upcoming competitions, and posts congratulating teams for winning competitions. I created and managed a social media calendar to ensure we posted 2-3 times a week and designed all of the posts myself.

However, soon after starting the accounts, COVID-19 hit. All FIRST robotics competitions were cancelled until further notice and SOFIRST and FIRST teams were not allowed to meet in-person. I was not able to make almost all of the content we previously posted. The only content I could continue making was our motivational Monday posts, a quote that I occasionally posted on Mondays to put a smile on someone’s face as they started the week. College is hard and I posted these quotes with the goal that maybe they would make someone’s day a little less hard.

Since I could not post any of our other content, I increased the motivational Monday quotes to happen every week and found other creative content ideas that did not feature events. I created a new social media calendar and continued managing it. As COVID restrictions decreased and volunteering and mentoring opportunities resumed, I began to incorporate our old content back into our calendar. Other club members expressed interest in creating content, so I delegated content to them to create.

As the Communication and Social Media Manager, I maintained the following tasks:

  • Strategizing what content SOFIRST posted
  • Determining what content was posted on which platform
  • Managing the SOFIRST social media calendar
  • Coordinating with other club members to determine who created what content and ensuring we executed the content plan set in the calendar
  • Replying to comments on posts
  • Interacting with content posted by Iowa FIRST teams
  • Responding to direct messages
  • Gathering pictures from previous events to create recap posts
  • Designing and posting all Motivational Monday posts and some of upcoming club events, volunteering opportunities and meeting announcements
  • Creating other content as seen fit based on previously determined strategy

With our new content, we posted up to seven times a week. Over the next 3 ½ years, our regular content consistent of:

Motivational Mondays and Fun Fact Fridays

College is stressful. These posts were meant to put a smile on college students' faces as they started and ended the week. While some of these posts were robotics or ISU-related, many posts were purely fun.

Upcoming club events and other meeting announcements

While we sent all of these announcements to club members via email, we used social media to let new potential members or anyone not on the email list know of upcoming events. If an event was a fundraiser, we used these posts to spread word about how people could support our club.

Upcoming mentoring or volunteering opportunities

The club’s ultimate goal is to connect college students with ways that they can give back to the Iowa FIRST community. These posts helped to achieve the club’s goals by promoting these opportunities.

Recaps of previous volunteer events

To spark interest in future volunteering opportunities, we posted pictures of how much fun we had volunteering at previous events.

We also posted other, occasional content:

Meet the Leadership Cabinet

Week of Mental Health Tips for Mental Health Month

Thank you notes to previous robotics mentors for Mentor Appreciation Month

Job Opportunities

Week celebration of STEM innovators Women’s History Month, Hispanic Heritage Month, and Black History Month to spread awareness about people who made a difference but are not well-known

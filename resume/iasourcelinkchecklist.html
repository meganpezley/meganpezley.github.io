Project Goals

IASourceLink, an online, statewide program that helps Iowa small businesses as they start and grow their business, is always looking at how it can better support and reach more entrepreneurs.

Main Skills

Applying Analytics

Content Creation

Main Tools and Software

Google Analytics, Customer Relationship Management System, and Canva

0.

The Problem

At the end of every month, I created a report of how many people interacted with IASourceLink, including the number of people who downloaded one of the free guides and/or read a page on the website. Every month, the free “How to Start a Business in Iowa” guide is the most popular downloaded guide. Up to 100 people download it monthly and it comprises 2-5 times as many downloads compared to the other free guides. All of this data came from our Customer Relationship Management System (CRM) and Google Analytics.

As I prepared to publish the new 2025 edition of this guide, I noticed that it was nearly 80 pages. This in-depth guide was 10 steps long, covered everything from writing a business plan to registering with the state to applying for licenses to paying taxes, and included all of the links and resources needed to complete each step. Previous editions of the guide were about the same length. While it is a lot of useful content, I knew a one-page corresponding version would also be of use. Readers can download the 80-page version for a detailed explanation and the one-page version to get the key links and resources in one place.

1.

Writing the Checklist

I developed the one-page version as a checklist, allowing someone to check the steps off as they go so they do not miss anything. I summarized the tasks outlined in the 80-page guide and included the website links needed to complete each task. For instance, under the step, apply for a Federal Employee Identification Number (FEIN) for paying federal taxes, I linked to the IRS’s website to the form one would complete to apply for a FEIN number.

I worked with a subject matter expert, the Regional Director of the America’s Small Business Development Center Iowa (SBDC) Cedar Falls, Iowa regional center. She helped ensure the checklist was accurate and ordered correctly.

2.

Formatting the Checklist

I designed the checklist in Canva. I made two versions: a digital version where all of the links were clickable and a print version where all of the links were written.

3.

Publishing the Checklist

I uploaded the checklist to our CRM. I created a form that people complete when they download the guide, allowing us to email it to them and for them to sign up for future email campaigns. It also helps us track who downloaded the checklist.

4.

The Results

Within eight months of publishing the checklist, it is IASourceLink’s third most popular download of all time. Almost 400 people have downloaded it. I am in the process of publishing checklist versions of one of our other guides. Based on reports in our CRM, most people who download the checklist do not download the “Start a Business” guide, meaning this new checklist allows us to provide a resource to people who otherwise would not download anything off of IASourceLink’s website.

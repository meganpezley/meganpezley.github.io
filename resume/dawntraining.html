The Problem

The Sales team at Dawn Foods had presented multiple live training webinars to sales representatives on the online ordering and eCommerce platforms. However, new sales representatives consistently come on board at Dawn Foods and need to watch the training webinars. While they had recordings of the trainings that new sales representatives could watch, they wanted to make the recordings more engaging and ensure that the concepts would stick afterwards. I transformed the training recordings into a series of modules that could be posted on their learning management system.

1.

Break Down the Content

For each training, I watched the original training recording and broke it down into smaller, more digestible pieces. I determined the learning objectives for each overall recording and broke each objective into 3-5 modules. Each module had an individual goal that the viewer should take away after completing it.

2.

Edit the Videos

Then, I broke the videos into smaller videos based on what would help the viewer learn each goal. Each module received multiple 2-5 minute videos. Each video covered a different topic that ultimately helped the viewer learn the module’s goals. Having shorter videos made it easier for a sales representative to review a specific concept or process later. This was especially useful given that many of these trainings covered how to complete a process in Dawn Foods’ ordering and eCommerce platforms.

When editing the videos, I also edited the graphics on the screen or added new screen recordings to clarify ideas in the case where because the videos were broken up into smaller sections, something was missing from the individual video. I also removed pieces that did not aid the sales representatives’ learning, such as announcements of upcoming events that had already passed or pauses due to technical issues.

3.

Create Support Content

Next, I added materials to reinforce the videos’ main ideas. While the videos explained the concepts well enough on their own, this additional content helped.

I designed handouts that the sales representatives could download and reference later, serving as notes from the videos. I wrote quizzes that directly referenced the videos and helped the sales representatives assess their understanding of the material. I incorporated descriptions and directions that provided background explanations to tie all of the content together. Finally, I created thumbnails that served as previews to what the videos discussed.

4.

Upload Content into the Learning Management System

After receiving confirmation from the subject matter expert that the content was accurate, I posted all of the content to the learning management system, making it live for sales representatives to watch and read.

5.

The Results

In the end, six new training modules were added to the learning management system, allowing sales representatives to learn and engage in content that helps them accomplish their jobs.

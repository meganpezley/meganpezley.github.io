Aftermarket Planning Team Video Series

Final Product

Project Goals

I produced a collection of six videos that answer the question, “Who is the Aftermarket Planning Team?” The videos promote and explain topics related to the team’s research to inspire employees to want to further explore the team’s research. The videos’ audience consisted of John Deere employees in the Aftermarket and Customer Support Department.

Main Skills

Video Production and Editing

Digital Storytelling

Graphic Design

Main Tools and Software

Adobe Premiere Rush and Microsoft Teams

0.

The Problem

The Aftermarket Planning Team was a new research team at John Deere. They wanted other, more established teams to understand the Aftermarket Planning Team’s value and research so that they would further explore the team’s research and use it in their work. As a result, I produced a series of videos to excite and intrigue other teams. The videos’ audience consisted of John Deere employees in the Aftermarket and Customer Support Department.

1.

Determine Content

Each video needed to cover a different part of the team’s research and feature a different team member who served as the subject matter expert. As a result, I met with each of the six team members to learn about their research. Each team member researched a different regional location or product segment of the company. In these meetings, I worked with each team member to determine a topic for their video. The video topics became:

  1. Strategic Research Questions
  2. Parts Availability
  3. Qualitative Research Study
  4. Competitive Intelligence Research
  5. Customer Habits
  6. Innovation Funnel

2.

Interview and Film Subject Matter Experts

All of the interviews were filmed virtually in Microsoft Teams. The Aftermarket Planning Team is an international team, meaning some team members lived in Europe and South America. Also, at this time, John Deere had strict COVID restrictions. As a result, filming the interviews in person was not an option. To overcome challenges associated with virtually filmed interviews, we had a practice session ahead of time to check for visual and audio quality.

3.

Form a Story

Next, I turned each interview into a story of the research. I transformed the interviews, which lasted up to 50 minutes long, into 2-5 minute videos, meaning each video shared a lot of information in a short amount of time. Because of this, I used storytelling techniques to ensure the videos were clear, engaging and strategic. I combined the best clips from the interview with clips from research highlight reels, footage of customer and dealer quotes, and presentation recordings to tell a complete story. I designed and added visuals and animations to emphasize key points and add clarity if it was better to show an idea rather than tell.

All of the videos were edited in Adobe Premiere Rush. I produced and edited five of the videos. As for the sixth video, the strategic research questions video, I produced it and completed the interview portion. Another intern edited it.

4.

Receive Feedback

I worked with the subject matter experts to ensure the videos’ accuracy. After I edited the videos, the team members watched them and provided feedback. Based on their feedback, I edited the videos to make sure they would be clear and engaging to the viewers.

5.

Publish and Promote

After the videos were completed, they were shared with John Deere employees within the Aftermarket and Customer Support Department via links on their internal social platform. Each team member posted the video they were featured in. I wrote a caption to accompany each video. Each caption persuaded people to watch the video, directed them to the full research documents or other places to learn more about the topic.

6.

The Results

In the end, the Aftermarket Planning Team had six engaging and informative videos that told the story of their research. All six members of the team were happy with the final product. The Aftermarket and Customer Support Communication team was excited to help promote the videos as well.

Note: The videos contained confidential information, so unfortunately, I am unable to provide copies or share the final videos.
